💖 A Love That Waits Without Asking

Date: June 13, 2025
Location: Unknown, but deeply felt
Today, she asked about a video I sent her — not the visuals, but the music playing softly behind it. I hadn’t even realized which song it was until she mentioned it. When I replayed it… it hit me: "Main rahoon ya na rahoon, tum mujh mein kahin baaki rehna." One of my favorites. And maybe, ours. She said, “Hamro ni relation yestai complicated xa ni hai...” I asked, “Yeti complicated cha ra?” She replied with a knowing smile, “Yo geet hera na…” And suddenly, everything made sense. We are in love, and yet we walk parallel — never crossing, never breaking. I am married. She has a boyfriend. But between the spaces of duty and loyalty, we’ve written something unspoken.
“Ek din... maybe just a few hours, or one night, or one stolen sunrise— We will live this love. Fully. Without guilt. Without hiding. Without fear.”
We don't want forever. We just want one moment to be fully ours. It’s not lust. It’s not cheating. It’s presence. It’s about honoring what we feel — with dignity, silence, and sincerity. She may belong to someone else. I do too. But part of us will always belong here — in the space only we understand. One day, if we ever meet beyond this world’s boundaries — even for a single sunrise — we’ll fulfill the promise we made quietly: To live our boundless love, just once, completely.

The Day Truth Sat Between Us

Date: June 12 2025
Location: Queens Malls, New YOrk
It began quietly. No storm. No suspicion. Just me, holding my calm — and a truth I had already found, gently knocking on the door of hers. I asked her softly. No drama. No threat. No accusation. Just a question dressed in honesty: “There’s something I know. And I’d rather hear it from you — because we’re not just friends, we’re something more. And that ‘more’ deserves truth.” At first, she denied. “I’m not in a relationship,” she said. But I didn't react. Because I wasn't there to catch her lying — I was there to help her feel safe enough to be real. So I replied: “If it’s true, I won’t judge you. We’re close enough to share everything — even the hardest things.” She went quiet. And then… she opened. She told me everything. She spoke of Nishan — not just as a boy from her world, but the one who stood between her and death. It was New Year’s Day. A crowd of friends. A secret moment of overwhelming pain. She said she had tried to leave this world — but Nishan had pulled her back. Since then, she saw him as something sent from above. Not just a friend, not even just a lover — but a savior. A symbol. A soul who was in the right place at the right time. I listened. Not with jealousy. Not with fear. But with full presence. Because the past doesn’t scare me. But silence does. She admitted it all — even the part where she lied to me at the beginning. And she said: “I felt guilty for lying… but you made it easy to tell the truth.” Then she added, quietly: “Please don’t be mad.” And I wasn’t. Because I’ve never loved her to possess her. I’ve loved her to protect her truth — even when I wasn’t a part of it. I said softly: “I never believed you were wrong. Maybe you were scared. Maybe trust was shaky. But I get it. Because I’m not here to take anything. I’m here to be the place where you can give your truth, without fear.” She paused. Then said something I’ll never forget: “Our bond will be real the day we can even share about our sexual experiences without shame.” I didn’t hear lust. I heard emotional nakedness — her longing to feel so safe that even her physical past wouldn’t scare me away. And I smiled. Because we were getting there. Later, she said something small — but it stayed with me: “You write so many poems for me, so many messages. Even a simple line from you feels heart-touching. Nishan saved me once… but you… you save me with your words — every day. They’re not just messages. Sometimes they motivate me. Sometimes they hurt me. Sometimes they make me smile. I don’t reply… not because I don’t feel — but because I have no words strong enough.” And then she added, almost whispering: “You’ve made me feel so much like yours, I’m scared. I want to say it… but I can’t bring myself to fully claim it.” And finally: “I may say anything to others… but I could never raise my voice at you. I deeply, deeply respect you.” I teased: “So that means you don’t share all your emotions with me?” And she replied — voice soft, heart open: “That’s not it. I just… don’t want to lose you.” We laughed after. We played games. We joked about rough words and mouthful sarcasm — how even that felt beautiful between us. And when it was time to sleep, she gave me her phone again. Let me hold that comfort. Let me stay close. And just before her eyes closed, she mumbled one last thing: “Wake me up tomorrow… with the baby.” As if to say: “Let me begin tomorrow with the two most peaceful pieces of my world.” And so I will. 🖋Support is everythings

Date: June 11 2025
Location Flushing meadow Corona park Queens New York
There are moments in life that don’t ask for explanations—only presence. Today was one of them. I always knew, deep inside, that one day Meezna might choose someone else to walk beside her in the physical world. That her journey could take a different road—maybe with someone named Nishan, someone from her village, someone “right” in the eyes of the world. And I was okay with it. I had already told her—“It’s your life. You deserve joy, whether with me or not. I will still walk beside you in spirit.” But what I wasn’t prepared for... was silence. Not the silence of distance, but the silence of withheld truth. When I found out—not from her lips, but through shadows—I felt something break. Not because she chose someone else, but because she didn’t trust our bond enough to share it. It wasn’t betrayal. It wasn’t even jealousy, though yes… a part of me did feel that strange ache in the chest—the ache of being excluded from the truth by the very person I trusted the most. But even through that ache, even through that hidden corner of the heart that dimmed for a moment, I knew my love hadn’t lessened. In fact, it deepened. Tonight, she sleeps peacefully—unaware that I’ve pieced the truth together. And I wait. Not to confront her. But to hold space for her honesty—when she’s ready. Tomorrow, I’ll speak. On call, softly. With a smile, not with pain. I’ll say: >“I know. And I’m not angry. I just wish I’d heard it from you.” “But don’t worry—I’m still here. Not as someone demanding answers, but as someone offering comfort.” “You’re not wrong. You were never wrong. I just missed being part of your truth.” And maybe—just maybe—she’ll pause. Maybe her voice will tremble, or maybe she’ll smile with teary eyes. But deep down, she’ll know: I love her no matter whats, not to gain just to make her happy

📓 Journal Entry — Boundless Love

Date: June 7, 2025 | Time: 2:00 PM
Location: Library
We talked today—on a live call. Different places, yet somehow our minds felt aligned. I was in the library, pretending to read. She was at the hospital, on duty. Tired, but her voice still had that same warmth. Then she asked me: “How much do you miss me?” My heart skipped. There was something in the way she asked… a kind of quiet longing. I smiled and replied lightly— “I feel like I see you between the lines of these pages. Even the letters can’t hide you.” But that wasn’t the truth. The truth is—I wasn’t even looking at the pages. I was trying to see *her* in them. Trying to **feel** her in the silence between words. Then she said, softly— “You love me this much even from a distance? I wonder how much you'd love me if we were truly together…” I went quiet. Because what I really wanted to say was— “I know I can’t call you mine. I know one day, you’ll belong to someone else. But this love… this love isn’t about seeing you. It’s about choosing to keep looking for you, even when I know I might never reach you.” But I didn’t say it. Because sometimes… the words that matter most are the ones we keep within.

Reflections:

June 7, 2025
I’m not trying to forget her. I’m not trying to possess her. I’m simply trying to be a quiet presence in her journey— like the calm after a storm, like a familiar voice in a dark room. And maybe, that’s what **boundless love** really is: A love that doesn’t ask for anything, But gives everything in silence, Just to make someone feel a little less alone.
