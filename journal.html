📓 Journal Entry — Boundless Love

Date: June 7, 2025 | Time: 2:00 PM
Location: Library
We talked today—on a live call. Different places, yet somehow our minds felt aligned. I was in the library, pretending to read. She was at the hospital, on duty. Tired, but her voice still had that same warmth. Then she asked me: “How much do you miss me?” My heart skipped. There was something in the way she asked… a kind of quiet longing. I smiled and replied lightly— “I feel like I see you between the lines of these pages. Even the letters can’t hide you.” But that wasn’t the truth. The truth is—I wasn’t even looking at the pages. I was trying to see *her* in them. Trying to **feel** her in the silence between words. Then she said, softly— “You love me this much even from a distance? I wonder how much you'd love me if we were truly together…” I went quiet. Because what I really wanted to say was— “I know I can’t call you mine. I know one day, you’ll belong to someone else. But this love… this love isn’t about seeing you. It’s about choosing to keep looking for you, even when I know I might never reach you.” But I didn’t say it. Because sometimes… the words that matter most are the ones we keep within.

Reflections:

June 7, 2025
I’m not trying to forget her. I’m not trying to possess her. I’m simply trying to be a quiet presence in her journey— like the calm after a storm, like a familiar voice in a dark room. And maybe, that’s what **boundless love** really is: A love that doesn’t ask for anything, But gives everything in silence, Just to make someone feel a little less alone.
