Boundless love page
Today, I looked at her face longer than usual. NOt just because we were on video call, but because, in that movement, she felt deeply mine -- so gentle, so real, so calm. Her long black hair felt soflty across her cheek like a light breeze. The blush on her face wasn't makeup -- it was emotion, alive and visible. Her eyes -- not very big, but deep, It felt like they were speaking without words. I saw a silent calm, a soft peace, and sometimes a question trying to rise. And her lips -- they parted slighty as she spoke, with a soft, natural pink tone. No shine from lipstic, no layer of gloss -- just pure, untouched beauty. There was a softness in them that made me pause. That simplicity that unfiltered smile -- It touched my heart. Her lips look like a poem written in silence, like the first page of a new story -- plain yet full of meaning. SHe has a braces on her teeth, and instead of taking away from her charm, they make her smile feel like a promise. LIke something still growing into it's bloom. It felt like her smile carries the dream of tomorrow. And then her nose Honestly, the world may not label it as "perfect" by beauty standards. But in that small imperfections, I found something deeply beutiful, honestly simplicity, and a sense of being real. That nose made her more human, more unique, more her. Mayve beauty isn't about symmetry -- maybe it's aboput soul. And today, without even trying she showed me hers.

BUt she reminded me that real beauty doesn't need fixing. She may not be fl;awless in every way, but she's true in a way that matters more. Looking at her today, I realised: the most beautiful things are often the simplest. The ones that speak directly to your heart without noise, without decoration.
